### 2. Monthly Spending Trend (Bar Chart)

![Monthly Trend Chart](../images/monthly-trend-chart.png)

**What you'll see**: A bar chart showing your spending for the last 6 months

#### How to Read It

- **X-axis** (bottom): Month names (Jan, Feb, Mar, etc.)
- **Y-axis** (left side): Dollar amounts
- **Blue bars**: How much you spent that month
- **Taller bar** = More spending
- **Shorter bar** = Less spending

#### Interacting with the Chart

1. **Hover over a bar** to see the exact amount
2. Popup shows: Month name and total amount

#### What to Look For

‚úÖ **Good Signs**:
- Bars getting shorter over time (spending less)
- Consistent bar heights (predictable spending)

‚ö†Ô∏è **Watch Out For**:
- Sudden spikes (investigate what caused them)
- Steadily increasing bars (spending trending up)

#### Example Interpretation

```
Jan  Feb  Mar  Apr  May  Jun
 |    ||   |    |||  |    ||
```

- March had lowest spending üéâ
- April was highest (what happened?)
- Overall trend is relatively stable

### 3. Spending by Category (Pie Chart)

![Category Pie Chart](../images/category-pie-chart.png)

**What you'll see**: A colorful pie chart showing where your money goes

#### Understanding the Colors

- üü¢ **Green** = Food
- üîµ **Blue** = Transportation
- üü£ **Purple** = Entertainment
- üéÄ **Pink** = Shopping
- üü° **Yellow** = Bills
- ‚ö´ **Gray** = Other

#### How to Read It

- **Bigger slice** = More money spent in that category
- **Smaller slice** = Less money spent
- **Percentage shown**: What portion of total spending

#### Example

If your pie chart shows:
- Food: 35% (biggest slice)
- Bills: 30%
- Transportation: 20%
- Shopping: 10%
- Entertainment: 5%

**Interpretation**: You spend most on food and bills. Entertainment is your smallest expense.

#### Using This Information

1. **Identify your biggest expense** (largest slice)
2. **Ask yourself**: "Is this where I want my money going?"
3. **Set goals**: "I'll try to reduce Food from 35% to 30% next month"
4. **Track progress**: Watch the pie chart change over time

## Common Questions

**Q: Why is my dashboard empty?**
A: You haven't added any expenses yet. Click "Add Sample Data" to see how it works, or start adding real expenses.

**Q: The pie chart only shows one color**
A: All your expenses are in one category. Try categorizing expenses more specifically.

**Q: My monthly trend only shows one bar**
A: You've only tracked expenses for one month. Keep adding expenses and you'll see trends over time.

**Q: The percentage change isn't showing**
A: You need expenses from at least two months for comparison.

**Q: Can I change the date range for charts?**
A: Not currently. The bar chart always shows last 6 months.

**Q: How often does the dashboard update?**
A: Instantly! As soon as you add, edit, or delete an expense, all charts update immediately.

## What to Watch For

### Red Flags üö©

1. **Constantly increasing bars** in monthly trend
   - Spending is trending upward
   - Time to review and cut back

2. **Huge spike** in one month
   - Investigate what caused it
   - Was it one-time or recurring?

3. **One category dominates** pie chart (>50%)
   - Most money going to one area
   - Consider if this is intentional

4. **This Month showing big increase** (+20% or more)
   - Spending significantly more than last month
   - Review recent expenses

### Green Flags üü¢

1. **Decreasing trend** in monthly bars
   - Successfully reducing spending
   - Keep it up!

2. **Balanced pie chart**
   - Money distributed across categories
   - Diversified spending

3. **Consistent monthly amounts**
   - Predictable, controlled spending
   - Easy to budget

4. **This Month showing decrease**
   - Spending less than last month
   - Great progress!
